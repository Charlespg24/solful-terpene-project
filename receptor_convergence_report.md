# Receptor Convergence Analysis: Solful Pre-Loading Strategy

*23 strains from 14 farms | 15 receptor targets | 3 Receptor Rounding Blends*

## Executive Summary

This analysis evaluates receptor coverage across Solful's 23-strain collection
and introduces **Receptor Rounding Blends** — three purpose-built mini-blends
that activate universally missing receptor pathways WITHOUT modifying any strain recipe.

**Core finding:** Every strain blend stays 100% intact. The rounding blends are a
separate, earlier step in the ritual — a pharmacological warm-up. They also work
as standalone aromatherapy products for specific intents (focus, pain, calm)
independent of any cannabis protocol.

**The three Receptor Rounding Blends:**
- **Clarity Round** (Rosemary + Eucalyptus, 5 drops) — AChE + BDNF-TrkB + TRPV3
- **Calm Round** (Peppermint + Clove, 3 drops) — TRPM8 + TRPA1 + COX-2
- **Ease Round** (Lavender, 3 drops) — GABA-A convergence (olfactory-only)

## 2. Receptor Rounding Blends

Three purpose-built mini-blends that fill receptor gaps across ALL 23 strains.
Each works as a **standalone aromatherapy product** OR as **Step 1 in the pre-loading protocol**.
Strain blends are never modified.

### Clarity Round

*"Sharp mind, clear recall"*

**Intent:** Cognitive sharpness, memory support, neuroprotection

**Recipe:** Rosemary ct. cineole (3d) + Eucalyptus (2d) = **5 drops**

**Receptors Opened:**

- **AChE** (enzyme) — inhibitor
  Preserves acetylcholine → memory and attention. Counters THC-induced short-term memory impairment (Russo 2011 entourage hypothesis).
  *Gap: 17/23 strains (74%) missing this pathway*
- **BDNF-TrkB** (growth_factor) — upregulator
  Neuroprotective signaling. Restores CREB phosphorylation in hippocampus.
  *Gap: 17/23 strains (74%) missing this pathway*
- **TRPV3** (TRP) — modulator
  Warm-temperature sensing and skin signaling.
  *Gap: 23/23 strains (100%) missing this pathway*

**Convergence:** Rosemary delivers DUAL AChE inhibition (eucalyptol + pinene). This is the only rounding blend with two terpenes targeting the same enzyme — true pharmacological stacking.

**Standalone Use:**
- Morning focus sessions before work
- Study or deep reading
- Creative problem-solving
- Counteracting brain fog (any cause)
- Pre-meeting mental sharpness

**Aroma:** Clean and invigorating — like walking through a Mediterranean herb garden
**Clash Risk:** Very low. Herbal freshness complements virtually all strain blends.

**Safety:**
- Rosemary: avoid during pregnancy and with epilepsy
- Eucalyptus: not for use around children under 6
- Both oils are food-grade at these concentrations

### Calm Round

*"Cool body, quiet pain"*

**Intent:** Cold-receptor analgesia, pain desensitization, physical comfort

**Recipe:** Peppermint (2d) + Clove (1d) = **3 drops**

**Receptors Opened:**

- **TRPM8** (TRP) — agonist
  Cold receptor activation → analgesic cooling sensation. Cross-desensitizes TRPV1 (heat/pain receptor), reducing pain sensitivity.
  *Gap: 23/23 strains (100%) missing this pathway*
- **TRPA1** (TRP) — agonist
  Irritant/pain receptor. Initial activation followed by desensitization → net analgesic effect. TRPA1 antagonism associated with anxiolytic effects.
  *Gap: 23/23 strains (100%) missing this pathway*
- **COX-2** (enzyme) — inhibitor
  Anti-inflammatory enzyme inhibition (same target as ibuprofen). Eugenol provides this as a bonus pathway.
  *Gap: 8/23 strains (35%) missing — bonus coverage*

**Convergence:** Menthol's TRPM8 activation cross-desensitizes TRPV1, which means if the strain already has myrcene (a TRPV1 modulator), the Calm Round creates a complementary pain-modulation stack: menthol cools → TRPV1 desensitizes → myrcene modulates the desensitized receptor.

**Standalone Use:**
- Post-workout muscle recovery
- Headache or tension relief
- Menstrual cramp comfort
- General body ache soothing
- Cold/flu season nasal relief
- Pre-massage muscle preparation

**Aroma:** The sensation of stepping from a warm spice kitchen into crisp mountain air
**Clash Risk:** Moderate. Mint and clove are assertive. Best used in a separate diffuser or inhaler, not mixed into the strain blend.

**Safety:**
- Peppermint: can be overwhelming — never exceed 2 drops in a 5-drop blend
- Clove: can cause irritation at high doses — 1 drop max
- Both oils are food-grade at these concentrations
- Avoid Calm Round if using topical capsaicin products (TRPV1 interaction)

### Ease Round

*"Soft landing, quiet mind"*

**Intent:** GABA-A convergence for anxiolysis, sedation, and emotional softening

**Recipe:** Lavender (3d) = **3 drops**

**Receptors Opened:**

- **GABA-A** (ion_channel) — positive_allosteric_modulator
  The primary inhibitory neurotransmitter receptor. Linalool binds the benzodiazepine site (allosteric), enhancing GABA's own effect without replacing it. When combined with myrcene (direct GABA-A modulator, present in 91% of strains), this creates SUPRA-ADDITIVE anxiolysis.
  *Gap: Only 2/23 strains missing GABA-A entirely, BUT 14/23 have Myrcene without Linalool — convergence opportunity*

**Convergence:** This is the strongest pharmacological case for cross-terpene pre-loading. Allosteric + direct modulation on the same receptor = 1.3x supra-additive bonus. Linalool primes the benzodiazepine site; when myrcene arrives (via strain blend), GABA transmission is amplified beyond what either terpene achieves alone.

**Standalone Use:**
- Pre-sleep wind-down routine
- Anxiety or panic moment relief
- Meditation or breathwork sessions
- Stressful day decompression
- Travel anxiety (airplane, hotel room)
- Partner/couple relaxation ritual

**Aroma:** The scent of a lavender field at dusk — immediately recognizable, universally soothing
**Clash Risk:** Very low. Lavender is the most universally compatible essential oil. It softens and rounds any blend without dominating.

**Safety:**
- Lavender is among the safest essential oils — well tolerated by virtually everyone
- MUST be diffused or inhaled, NOT ingested for this purpose
- Olfactory habituation begins at ~30 minutes — don't exceed 30 min pre-load
- If using Silexan capsules (oral), note this activates a DIFFERENT pathway than olfactory — both can be used but they work through separate mechanisms

### The Pre-Loading Protocol

**Step 1: Receptor Rounding** (10-15 minutes before Step 2)
- Method: Separate diffuser, personal inhaler, or steam bowl
- Choose 1-2 rounding blends based on session intent. Add drops to diffuser and begin inhaling. This opens receptor pathways that the strain blend doesn't cover.
- **Intent-based selection:**
  - Focus → Clarity Round
  - Pain → Calm Round
  - Anxiety → Ease Round
  - Full Spectrum → Clarity + Calm (8 drops total)
  - Deep Relaxation → Ease + Calm (6 drops total)

**Step 2: Strain Blend** (5-10 minutes after starting Step 1 (overlap is fine))
- Method: Personal inhaler, second diffuser, or topical application
- Apply your existing 40-drop strain-matched blend exactly as designed. Do not modify the recipe. The rounding blend and strain blend work on SEPARATE receptor pathways — they don't compete.

**Step 3: Cannabis** (15-30 minutes after starting Step 1)
- Method: Consume the matched strain
- By this point, rounding receptors are primed (AChE inhibited, TRPM8 activated, GABA-A upregulated) and strain-matched terpene receptors are occupied. THC/CBD arrive to a pharmacologically prepared system.

### Standalone Use

Each rounding blend works as an independent aromatherapy product without cannabis or strain blends. The receptor targeting provides real therapeutic benefit on its own.

**Method:** Add drops to diffuser, personal inhaler, or dilute in carrier oil (jojoba, fractionated coconut) at 2-3% for topical use.
**Duration:** 15-40 minutes diffusion for olfactory effects; topical effects last 1-2 hours

## 5. Core Finding

### The Data Across 23 Solful Strains

- **Strategy A (Same-Terpene)** won for **0** strains
- **Strategy B (Receptor-Convergent)** won for **0** strains
- **Strategy C (Pathway-Diversified)** won for **23** strains

#### Key Finding: Pathway Diversification Dominates — But Context Matters

Strategy C wins most often because the average Solful strain covers only 3-5 of 15
known receptor targets. The marginal value of each new receptor pathway is high.

**However, this doesn't mean you should ignore same-terpene matching (Strategy A).**
Strategy A represents your existing oil recipe approach — and it's already well-designed.
The real question is: **what should you ADD to your existing recipes?**

#### The Pharmacological Answer

**Your existing oil recipes (Strategy A) are the foundation.** They correctly match
each strain's dominant terpenes to botanical sources. What receptor analysis reveals
is where to **supplement**:

1. **GABA-A is the convergence goldmine.** Three terpenes modulate it (Myrcene,
   Linalool, alpha-Bisabolol). When a strain has myrcene but lacks linalool, adding
   lavender creates allosteric + direct synergy (1.3x supra-additive bonus). This is
   the strongest pharmacological case for cross-terpene pre-loading.

2. **CB2 has no convergence option.** Only Caryophyllene activates CB2 (Ki=155nM).
   No cross-terpene can substitute. For CB2-mediated effects (anti-inflammatory,
   pain), same-terpene pre-loading via black pepper is the only path.

3. **AChE inhibition stacks.** Both alpha-Pinene and Eucalyptol inhibit AChE.
   For cognitive enhancement during cannabis, combining pine oil + rosemary
   creates dual AChE inhibition that could better counter THC memory effects.

    #### Best Receptor Convergence Example: Mule Fuel

    
#### The Optimal Hybrid Protocol for Solful Strains

1. **Use your existing oil recipe** (Strategy A) — it's your foundation
2. **Add ONE convergent terpene** if the strain hits a hotspot:
   - Strain has Myrcene but no Linalool? → Add 2-3 drops Lavender (GABA-A convergence)
   - Strain has alpha-Pinene? → Add 2-3 drops Rosemary ct. cineole (dual AChE)
   - Strain has Caryophyllene only for CB2? → Already covered, no convergence possible
3. **For narrow strains** (≤2 terpenes): consider adding a terpene that opens a
   new receptor pathway entirely (e.g., Eucalyptol for TRPV3/AChE)

#### Critical Caveats

1. **No human RCTs validate multi-terpene pre-loading.** Only Spindle 2024
   (limonene + THC) has human data.
2. **Linalool only works via olfactory pathway** — it must be inhaled, not ingested.
3. **Receptor saturation limits gains** — 2-AG already occupies ~50% of CB2.
4. **Placebo and aromatic expectancy effects are likely significant.**
5. **Max 2-3 supplementary drops** to avoid overwhelming the carefully designed
   blend ratios in your existing recipes.
