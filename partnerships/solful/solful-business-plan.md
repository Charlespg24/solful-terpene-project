## Executive Summary

Solful Sessions is a terpene science company that bridges three historically separated research traditions — cannabis pharmacology, clinical aromatherapy, and forest bathing immunology — into a unified platform for enhancing human experience through precision terpene formulation.

**The core insight:** Cannabis flower delivers terpenes at 100–10,000× below pharmacologically active doses. Essential oils deliver the same molecules at 20–950× higher concentrations. A β-caryophyllene molecule doesn't care if it comes from a joint or a pepper grinder — the receptor only sees the molecular shape.

**The timing innovation:** Pre-loading — breathing a strain-matched terpene blend 15–30 minutes before cannabis — primes receptor systems via A2a-CB1 heteromeric crosstalk. Terpenes before THC is pharmacologically different from terpenes with THC. Sequence matters.

## The Innovation

Three interlocking inventions protected by a single provisional patent application:

### 1. The Pre-Loading Method (Claims 1–14)

Temporally sequenced inhalation of strain-matched terpene blends 15–30 minutes before cannabis consumption. This primes A2a-CB1 heteromeric complexes via three parallel pathways: olfactory-limbic (onset in seconds), trigeminal-autonomic (1–2 minutes), and pulmonary-systemic (5–20 minutes).

### 2. The Matching Engine (Claims 15–22)

An algorithm that ingests COA lab data, extracts terpene ratios, queries an essential oil composition database of 18 ISO-standardized oils, and outputs a precise blend recipe within 15% terpene ratio tolerance.

### 3. Multisensory Experience Curation

Extends terpene matching to music (Spotify parameters derived from shared neurochemistry), beverages (120 recipes across 5 categories), lighting, breathwork, and environmental design.

### Key Scientific Breakthroughs (2024–2025)

**Schwarz et al., 2024 (PAIN):** Five cannabis terpenes produce analgesia equivalent to morphine via adenosine A2a receptors — not CB1/CB2.

**Spindle et al., 2024 (Drug & Alcohol Dependence):** d-Limonene dose-dependently attenuates THC-induced anxiety. First controlled human trial.

**Alayoubi et al., 2025:** Myrcene: dose-dependent analgesia in neuropathic pain, CB1-independent, A2a pathway.

**LaVigne et al., 2021 (Scientific Reports):** First experimental evidence for entourage effect mechanism. 108 citations, 185k+ accesses.

## IP & Patent Strategy

### The Cage: Three Claim Types

**Method claims** (what you do) — can't practice the pre-loading protocol without a license. **System claims** (what the software does) — can't build a COA-to-blend calculator without a license. **Composition claims** (the physical product) — can't sell a strain-matched EO blend without a license. Together they form a complete cage.

### Patent Portfolio: CPG-2026-PROV-001

**Family A — The Pre-Loading Method (Claims 1–14):** Breathing a strain-matched terpene blend before consuming cannabis to prime receptor systems. Independent claim covers the broad method; 13 dependents add time windows (5–60 min broad, 10–30 min preferred), A2a-CB1 heteromer mechanism, measurable modulation outcomes, terpene list (10 key terpenes), ISO essential oil embodiment, delivery devices, music integration with iso principle, beverage pairing, and breathwork protocol. Protects: Solful EO blends, La Séance events, Vibe Curator timer, any dispensary pre-loading product, clinical trial methodology.

**Family B — The Matching Engine (Claims 15–22):** Computer system that takes lab data and generates a blend recipe. 7 dependents add multisensory extension, validation algorithm, weighted audio targets, mobile/web/kiosk UI, inventory integration, and connected diffuser hardware (§101-safe). Protects: Vibe Curator app, any COA-to-blend calculator, dispensary kiosks, white-label licensing, connected diffusers.

**Family C — Non-Cannabis State Modulation (Claims 23–30):** Choosing a target mental state and breathing a formulated blend. No cannabis. 7 dependents add time window, activation/calm focus/recovery states, athletic context, music, beverage. Protects: Corporate focus blends, wellness studios, yoga/meditation.

**Family D — Sports Method-of-Treatment (Claims 31–39):** Three independent methods tying specific formulations to measurable outcomes. Dependents specify activation/recovery/anxiety blend details, delivery settings, expanded contexts (public speaking, exams). Protects: Sports teams, athletic products, performance anxiety products.

**Family E — Cannabis Compositions (Claims 40–43):** The physical blend itself — composition of matter. 3 dependents add preferred format (2mL, 30–50 drops), packaging with instructions, QR-connected experience. Protects: Solful bottles, any strain-matched EO product, white-label blends.

**Family F — Wellness Compositions (Claim 44):** Physical blends for state modulation with no cannabis association. Four predefined formulations. Protects: Sports line, corporate wellness, DTC wellness brand.
