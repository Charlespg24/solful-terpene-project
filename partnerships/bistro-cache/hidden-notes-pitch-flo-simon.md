## Terpenes: The Hidden Thread

Terpenes are aromatic compounds found in over 20,000 plant species. They're the reason cannabis strains feel different from one another — and they're the same molecules found in culinary herbs, citrus fruits, flowers, and essential oils.

**In Cannabis** — Terpenes determine why one strain energizes and another relaxes. They drive the experience more than THC alone.

**In Cuisine** — Black pepper, rosemary, lavender, lemongrass, citrus zest — the herbs and spices Simon already uses are terpene-rich.

**In Aromatherapy** — Essential oils work because of terpenes. Lavender calms, eucalyptus energizes — the same chemistry, the same molecules.

> *Same molecule. Same effect. Whether it comes from a cannabis flower or a sprig of rosemary.*

## Hidden Notes: A Five-Course Terpene Journey

Each course pairs Simon's cuisine with a specific terpene — matched through food, cocktail, aroma, and music. The essential oil diffusion shifts with each course. The playlist evolves. Tasting cards reveal which Solful strains share each terpene profile.

No cannabis is consumed at Caché. The terpenes are the bridge.

---

## Course I — The Awakening

**α-Pinene** — Pine, Rosemary, Eucalyptus

**The Plate:** Salmon Gravlax, Fennel & Blood Orange
Fennel is rich in α-pinene and limonene — a natural terpene bridge. Paired with rosemary crostini.

**The Glass:** Gin & Rosemary Fizz
Juniper-forward gin amplifies the pine. Rosemary sprig = pinene in the glass.

**The Air:** Pine + rosemary + eucalyptus essential oils. Alertness, mental clarity.

*Solful match: strains high in pinene (Blue Dream, Jack Herer profiles)*

---

## Course II — The Bloom

**Linalool** — Lavender, Basil, Tarragon

**The Plate:** Petrale Sole, Dry Vermouth & Tarragon
Tarragon contains linalool — calming, floral, elegant. Already one of Simon's signature preparations.

**The Glass:** Lavender French 75
Champagne, gin, lavender syrup, lemon. Light, aromatic, floral.

**The Air:** Lavender + basil essential oils. Calming, stress-relieving, elegant transition.

*Solful match: strains high in linalool (Lavender Kush, Do-Si-Dos profiles)*

---

## Course III — The Heart

**Myrcene** — Lemongrass, Thyme, Mango

**The Plate:** Mushroom Vol-Au-Vent, Thyme Reduction
Earthy mushrooms + thyme (rich in myrcene) create the deepest, most grounding course. Simon's vol-au-vent is already a guest favorite.

**The Glass:** Mango-Lemongrass Smash
Ripe mango + lemongrass + bourbon. Warming and tropical. Myrcene is why mangoes potentiate cannabis.

**The Air:** Lemongrass + thyme + bay leaf essential oils. Warming, sedating, intimate.

*Solful match: strains high in myrcene (OG Kush, Granddaddy Purple profiles)*

---

## Course IV — The Spice

**β-Caryophyllene** — Black Pepper, Clove, Cinnamon

**The Plate:** Hanger Steak, Black Pepper Sauce
β-caryophyllene is the only terpene that directly activates cannabinoid receptors. Black pepper is its richest culinary source. Simon already makes this dish — it's destiny.

**The Glass:** Mezcal & Black Pepper Old Fashioned
Smoky mezcal, cracked black pepper, mole bitters. Bold and complex — Flo's territory.

**The Air:** Black pepper + clove + cinnamon essential oils. Anti-inflammatory, warming, bold.

*Solful match: strains high in caryophyllene (GSC, Bubba Kush profiles)*

---

## Course V — The Glow

**Limonene** — Lemon, Orange, Grapefruit

**The Plate:** Pistachio Crème Brûlée, Blood Orange Zest
Citrus zest is pure limonene. Combined with pistachio — already on Simon's dessert menu — it's the perfect uplift to close the journey.

**The Glass:** Limoncello Spritz
Limoncello, prosecco, blood orange. Bright, celebratory, effervescent.

**The Air:** Lemon + sweet orange + grapefruit essential oils. Mood elevation, joy, afterglow.

*Solful match: strains high in limonene (Super Lemon Haze, Wedding Cake profiles)*

---

## The Return — Herbal Tea Finale

**All Five Terpenes Reunited**

A bespoke herbal tea containing rosemary (pinene), lavender (linalool), lemongrass (myrcene), black pepper (caryophyllene), and lemon verbena (limonene). The full journey in one cup.

As guests sip, each tasting card is turned over — revealing which Solful cannabis strains share the exact terpene profile of each course they just experienced.

Each guest receives a take-home terpene profile card with their matched Solful strains.

## The Full Sensory Picture

### Essential Oil Diffusion
A custom essential oil blend is prepared for each course and diffused throughout the dining room. As the menu evolves, so does the air — from bright pine and rosemary to warming black pepper and clove to uplifting citrus. Guests experience the terpene shift in every breath.

### Curated Spotify Playlist
The soundtrack follows the terpene arc. Course I opens bright and airy — bossa nova, light jazz. Course III deepens into soul and ambient textures. Course IV brings warmth with smoky, rhythmic selections. By Course V, the energy lifts — celebratory, warm, golden hour.

### Tasting Cards & Education
Beautiful printed cards at each setting — one per course. Front: the terpene name, its aroma wheel, and what it does. Back (hidden until dessert): the Solful strain that shares this exact profile, and what to ask for at the dispensary. Education disguised as elegance.
