# Moongazer Farms — Strain & Terpene Research

## About Moongazer Farms

Moongazer Farms is a small family-operated cannabis farm in **Redwood Valley, Mendocino County, California**. Their tagline — *"Sun Grown, Seed Sown"* — captures their core philosophy: every plant is grown outdoors from seed (not clones) in living soil built through regenerative and biodynamic practices.

They hold dual certifications — **Demeter Biodynamic** and **Dragonfly Earth Medicine Pure** — and won the **2016 Emerald Cup Regenerative Cannabis Farm Award**. Their approach goes beyond organic: they build no-till living soil using hugelkultur, integrate polyculture plantings (lavender, comfrey, nettle, mushrooms), and manage their oak woodlands with goats and chickens. The result is what they call "carbon-sequestering cannabis."

Growing from seed rather than clones means more genetic diversity in their harvests — each plant expresses its own phenotype, which creates natural variation in terpene profiles and effects. This is a fundamentally different approach from the clone-driven consistency most commercial cultivators pursue.

## Strain Profiles

### 1. Moonlight

**Type:** Details on Moongazer's specific Moonlight cut are limited in public databases.

**General Profile (Moonlight lineage):**
- THC: ~12–16%
- Dominant terpenes: Myrcene, Phellandrene, Humulene, β-Caryophyllene
- Aroma/Flavor: Pine, butter, sage, cheese, spicy pepper
- Effects: Euphoria, chattiness, relaxation, appetite stimulation

**Terpene Notes:** The myrcene-humulene backbone here is interesting — myrcene is the classic sedative/couch-lock terpene, while humulene (its molecular isomer) is actually appetite-suppressant and anti-inflammatory. The phellandrene adds a minty-citrus dimension that's underappreciated in cannabis profiles.

### 2. Natty Bumppo

**Genetics:** Double OG Chem × (5Gs Yellow × OG Sour) F3
**Breeder:** Rebel Grown
**Type:** Indica-dominant hybrid

**Profile:**
- THC: **33%+** (41% total cannabinoids reported)
- Total terpenes: **3%+**
- Aroma/Flavor: Gas fuel, rotten fruit tang, funk — extremely loud
- Effects: Balanced relaxation with clear-headed mental clarity; good for afternoon/evening use

**Awards:**
- 7th place, 2022 Emerald Cup
- 8th place, 2023 Emerald Cup

**Terpene Notes:** At 3%+ total terpenes with 33%+ THC, this is a powerhouse. The OG Chem lineage typically delivers heavy β-caryophyllene and limonene with myrcene underneath. The "gas fuel" descriptor is classic caryophyllene-dominant expression. The "rotten fruit" note suggests significant amounts of farnesene or certain esters that develop in high-terpene cultivars during the cure.

### 3. Black Lime Chem

**Genetics:** Woodman Canyon Oil Can × Lime Afghani × Northern Lights × Purple Kush × Chemdawg Special Reserve
**Breeder:** Aficionado Seeds
**Type:** Indica-dominant hybrid

**Profile:**
- THC: 18–25% (some tests up to 26%)
- CBD: ~1–2%
- **Dominant terpenes:**
  - **d-Limonene:** 0.5–1.2%
  - **β-Caryophyllene:** 0.4–1.0%
  - **Myrcene:** 0.3–0.8%
  - **Humulene:** 0.1–0.3%
  - **β-Ocimene:** 0.1–0.3%
- Total terpenes: 1.5–3.0%
- Aroma/Flavor: Lime peel brightness, pepper, clove, subtle amaro-like bitterness, earthy and resinous
- Effects: Relaxing, pain relief, mood elevation

**Terpene Notes:** This is a fascinating terpene cocktail. The limonene-caryophyllene combination creates what's sometimes called the "citrus diesel" profile — limonene hits the serotonin and adenosine pathways (mood, alertness) while caryophyllene activates CB2 receptors directly (anti-inflammatory, anxiolytic). The presence of β-ocimene at 0.1–0.3% is notable — it's one of the more volatile monoterpenes and contributes to that bright, almost tropical top-note that distinguishes this from a standard chem profile. The "lime peel" descriptor maps almost perfectly to the limonene + ocimene combination.

The 1–2% CBD is also significant for the entourage effect — even small amounts of CBD modulate THC's binding at CB1, potentially smoothing out the experience.

### 4. Raspberry Danish

**Genetics:** Lemon Diesel × Banana Jam
**Type:** Balanced hybrid (50/50 Indica/Sativa)

**Profile:**
- THC: 17–20%
- Aroma/Flavor: Creamy vanilla pastry, fruity raspberry, spicy banana, fresh tree fruits
- Effects: Slow-building, creeping high; deeply calming and relaxing; eases both physical and mental tension
- Commonly recommended for: Chronic stress, anxiety, chronic pain, depression

**Terpene Notes:** The "pastry/doughy" descriptors in cannabis almost always trace back to significant linalool content, often combined with certain volatile esters. The Lemon Diesel parent brings a fuel-forward limonene + caryophyllene base, while Banana Jam likely contributes isoamyl acetate (the literal "banana" compound) plus myrcene. The raspberry fruitiness could come from geraniol or certain terpinolene expressions.

The balanced 50/50 genetics at a moderate 17–20% THC with this terpene profile makes it a really approachable strain — the kind of thing that works for both experienced and newer consumers. The "slow creeping" onset is typical of balanced terpene profiles where no single terpene dominates the initial experience.

## Terpene Pre-Loading Relevance

Connecting this to the terpene project — Moongazer's strains offer interesting test cases for pre-loading protocols:

**Black Lime Chem** is the standout candidate. Its well-documented terpene profile (limonene-dominant with caryophyllene and ocimene) maps cleanly to botanical sources: lime essential oil (limonene + ocimene), black pepper oil (caryophyllene), and hops (humulene). A pre-loading blend could be constructed from these three oils to prime the relevant receptor pathways (CB2 via caryophyllene, serotonin via limonene, adenosine A2a via the terpene mix) 15–30 minutes before consumption.

**Natty Bumppo** at 3%+ total terpenes is already delivering significant terpene doses directly — the question is whether pre-loading could enhance the experience further or whether there's a ceiling effect at that concentration.

**Raspberry Danish** with its balanced, moderate profile might actually be the best *consumer-facing* pre-loading demonstration — moderate THC + approachable terpenes + slow onset = more room to perceive the pre-loading effect.
