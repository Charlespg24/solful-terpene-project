## 1. SERIES IDENTITY

**Name:** Solful Sessions — The Terpene Files
**Host:** Charles Pelletier-Gagné
**Tagline:** *The same molecule. Every plant. One body.*
**Format:** Solo monologue, AI-narrated (ElevenLabs voice clone), 20–25 minutes per episode
**Release cadence:** Biweekly
**Tone:** Smart friend at a dinner party who happens to have spent a decade studying this. Personal, curious, grounded in science but never clinical. Uses story to carry pharmacology.
**Core thesis:** Terpene source independence — the same molecule activates the same receptor regardless of botanical origin. Cannabis, aromatherapy, forest bathing, food, and perfumery are studying the same pharmacology through different lenses.
**Differentiator:** No other podcast treats the terpene molecule as the protagonist across all botanical sources. Everyone else stays in their silo (cannabis OR aromatherapy OR forest bathing). This show walks across all of them.

## 2. EPISODE TYPES

### Type A — Single Terpene Deep-Dive
Input: Terpene monograph. Output: Single-molecule portrait covering origin, pharmacology, cultural history, cannabis connection, pre-loading protocol.

### Type B — Strain Profile
Input: Strain COA + farm context + personal experience. Output: Strain portrait told through its terpene ensemble. Each terpene is a character; the profile is the story. Includes essential oil recipe, beverage pairing, music, and ritual context.

### Type C — Terpene Comparison
Input: Two or more terpenes with overlapping or contrasting pharmacology. Output: Side-by-side exploration.

### Type D — Farm Series
Input: Multiple strains from one farm. Output: Farm philosophy + daily arc showing how one soil and one philosophy produce different terpene expressions across strains.

## 3. SEVEN-BEAT EPISODE ARCHITECTURE

Every episode follows this sequence. Beats expand or compress, but the order is fixed: sensory → scientific → cultural → practical.

| Beat | Duration | Purpose | Rules |
|------|----------|---------|-------|
| **COLD OPEN** | 60–90 sec | Hook with a sensory scene. No jargon. Name the molecule at the END as a reveal. | First sentence = place, moment, or question — never a definition. Present tense. |
| **THE MOLECULE** | 2–3 min | Make the molecule tangible. Chemical identity as human-scale story. Botanical sources as geography. | Lead with the most surprising fact. Translate numbers into sensory terms. |
| **THE SCIENCE** | 3–5 min | Pharmacology for the curious. 2–3 mechanisms. Receptor → pathway → experience chain. | Lead with strongest evidence, not most famous claim. State evidence tier honestly. Max 90 sec of unbroken science before a narrative beat. Always name who did the study. |
| **THE BRIDGE** | 2–3 min | Cultural history. Food, drink, medicine, ceremony, place. | At least two domains. Frame ancient use as "they didn't know the chemistry, but they knew the effect." |
| **CANNABIS CONNECTION** | 2–3 min | How this terpene shows up in cannabis. Strains as case studies. | Name 3–5 real strains. Connect user reports back to pharmacology. State source independence naturally. |
| **THE PRE-LOAD** | 2–3 min | Practical protocol. Essential oils, methods, timing. | Name specific oils. Acknowledge this is the frontier. Make it actionable. State bioavailability constraints honestly. |
| **THE CLOSE** | 60–90 sec | Return to cold open scene. Recontextualize with everything they now know. | Circle back to Beat 1. End on source independence. Final sentence should be quotable. |

**Type B Strain Adaptation:** Cold Open = experience of smoking this strain. The Molecule → The Profile (terpenes as ensemble cast). The Science → interactions and entourage for this ratio. The Bridge → matching essential oils, foods, settings. Cannabis Connection → deep-dive on this farm/lineage/region. The Pre-Load → strain-matched essential oil recipe walkthrough. The Close → the intent of the strain.

## 4. VOICE & STYLE RULES

**DO:** First person for personal experience. "You" to address listener. Mix short punchy sentences with flowing ones. Name real researchers, studies, places, products. Use metaphor to carry pharmacology. Acknowledge uncertainty. Vary rhythm: fact-story-fact-question-story-fact.

**DON'T:** Open with a definition. Use passive voice for more than one sentence. Stack 3+ scientific terms without translation. Say "studies show" without attribution. Hedge excessively. Use "actually." End with generic CTA.

**VOCABULARY:** Use freely: terpene, cannabinoid, receptor, pathway, entourage effect, pre-loading, botanical, volatile. Translate on first use: acetylcholinesterase, BDNF, NF-κB, NK cells. Avoid in monologue: IC₅₀, IUPAC nomenclature, CAS numbers, statistical notation (save for show notes).

**EVIDENCE TRANSPARENCY LANGUAGE:**
- "This is proven in humans" = RCT or strong human data
- "This is demonstrated in animal models" = preclinical
- "This is shown in vitro" = cell/enzyme assays
- "This is a hypothesis — mechanistically sound but unproven" = theoretical
- "This was tested and didn't hold up" = negative result (state honestly)

## 9. CRITICAL SCIENTIFIC CONTEXT

### What the Hopkins Lab Has Proven and Disproven

| Terpene | Claim | Result | Citation |
|---------|-------|--------|----------|
| d-Limonene | Reduces THC-induced anxiety | **POSITIVE** — dose-dependent, selective, no interference with other THC effects | Spindle et al. 2024, Drug Alcohol Depend. |
| α-Pinene | Protects against THC-induced memory impairment | **NEGATIVE** — no evidence at doses tested | Kumar et al. 2025, Med. Cannabis Cannabinoids |

**Upcoming from Hopkins:** Oral limonene replication. Myrcene sedation study. Patent filed: PCT/US2022/014296 (limonene for THC anxiety).

### The A2a Receptor Discovery (Schwarz/Streicher, Arizona)
Five cannabis terpenes produce morphine-equivalent antinociception in neuropathic pain models via adenosine A2a receptors in spinal cord (NOT CB2). Confirmed by: istradefylline blockade, CRISPR knockdown, in vitro cAMP, molecular docking. No reward liability. Myrcene identified as primary A2a analgesic driver; limonene excluded. Inhalation route did NOT produce antinociception in mice (important limitation for pre-loading).

### Key Principle: Evidence-Tiered Honesty
This show's credibility is built on stating what works, what doesn't, and what we don't know yet. The pinene negative result and the limonene positive result from the same lab = the most powerful proof that this show tells the whole story. "If I told you only the Russo hypothesis and left out the Hopkins data, I'd sound like every other cannabis podcast."
