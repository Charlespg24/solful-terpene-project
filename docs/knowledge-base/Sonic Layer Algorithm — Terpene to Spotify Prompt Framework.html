The Pipeline

Every strain carries a terpene fingerprint — a ratio of aromatic molecules that determines not just how it smells, but how it feels. This framework transforms that chemical fingerprint into a Spotify prompt by passing it through six sequential stages. Each stage narrows the musical space until you have a prompt specific enough for Spotify's AI to generate a coherent playlist that matches the strain's experiential arc.

Stage 1: Terpene → Energy Classification

The dominant terpene sets the gravitational center of the playlist, but the secondary and tertiary terpenes modify the direction. Think of it like a chord: the root note is the dominant terpene, and the other terpenes add color. The reported effect (from farm or dispensary notes) acts as a tiebreaker when the terpene profile is ambiguous.

1

Identify the Dominant Terpene

The terpene with the highest percentage sets the base energy. If two terpenes are within 15% of each other, treat the profile as "balanced" and lean on the reported effect to classify.

2

Apply Modifier Terpenes

Secondary terpenes push the base energy up or down. Limonene lifts any base. Myrcene pulls any base toward sedation. Pinene adds clarity/alertness. Linalool smooths edges. Ocimene adds cerebral brightness. Bisabolol adds gentle warmth.

3

Check for Paradox Strains

Some strains defy their terpene profile (e.g., Avenue of the Giants: 1.94% myrcene but energizing). When the reported effect contradicts the terpene prediction, trust the effect and let the terpene profile shape the texture instead of the energy level.

4

Assign to Energy Category

Map the result to one of 8 categories (see table below). Each category has preset BPM ranges, energy levels, vocal rules, and arc shapes.

Stage 2: The Eight Energy Categories

Each category is a complete musical template with preset parameters. Once you classify a strain, these parameters give you the skeleton of the prompt. The terpene profile then fine-tunes within these ranges.

Deep Rest / Sleep
ENERGY 0.05–0.25

BPM: Start 75–80, descend to 55 or below

Duration: 90 minutes

Arc: Iso principle — start where the listener is, then gradually descend. Remove percussion by midpoint. Approach silence by the end.

Vocals: None. Instrumental only.

Texture: Warm darkness, weighted blanket, sub-bass, no sharp edges.

Triggers: Myrcene >1%, or heavy caryophyllene + farnesene, or effect = "sedative/sleepy/heavy"

Warm Relaxation
ENERGY 0.20–0.45

BPM: 75–95 steady

Duration: 75–90 minutes

Arc: Steady center with gentle late descent. Never rushed, never sleepy.

Vocals: Tender vocals in first half only, or none.

Texture: Analog warmth, candlelight, acoustic instruments, soft vibraphone, nylon guitar.

Triggers: Caryophyllene-dominant with moderate myrcene, or effect = "relaxed/calm/peaceful"

Contemplative Drift
ENERGY 0.30–0.50

BPM: 80–100 steady

Duration: 75–90 minutes

Arc: Wandering, dreamy, layered. Mind drifts while body stays settled.

Vocals: Minimal or soft first half only.

Texture: Hazy, golden, reverb-washed, layered samples, interesting details to notice.

Triggers: Limonene-dominant with linalool or caryophyllene close behind, or effect = "contemplative/creative/curious"

Body/Mind Split
ENERGY 0.35–0.55

BPM: 80–90

Duration: 75 minutes

Arc: Dual layer — heavy cushioned bottom with sparkling upper register.

Vocals: None. Instrumental only.

Texture: Velvet bass + crystalline highs. Jazz-electronic fusion. Lying on velvet watching fireworks.

Triggers: High myrcene + ocimene or linalool, or effect = "body relaxed + mind active"

Clear Focus
ENERGY 0.40–0.55

BPM: 95–105 steady

Duration: 60 minutes

Arc: Perfectly steady. Clean and level throughout.

Vocals: None.

Texture: Clean air, natural light, spacious, precise. Supports concentration without demanding attention.

Triggers: CBD strains, or terpinolene + pinene, or effect = "focused/clear/productive"

Easy Social
ENERGY 0.45–0.65

BPM: 90–115 (builds, then winds down)

Duration: 75–90 minutes

Arc: Conversational — build gently, peak in middle, warm wind-down.

Vocals: Yes. Real voices, human warmth.

Texture: Analog, live instruments, real mixes. Present but never demanding. Like good conversation.

Triggers: Caryophyllene-dominant + limonene or myrcene, or effect = "social/uplifting/happy"

Bright Uplift
ENERGY 0.60–0.80

BPM: 105–125

Duration: 60–70 minutes

Arc: Sustained high. Pure effervescence from start to finish.

Vocals: Welcome — feel-good hooks, clear vocals.

Texture: Bright synths, tight percussion, crisp production. Major keys only. Everything smiling.

Triggers: Limonene-dominant, or balanced bright profile, or effect = "euphoric/cheerful/energized"

Vivid Energy
ENERGY 0.65–0.85

BPM: 105–120+

Duration: 60–75 minutes

Arc: Propulsive build then hold. Forward momentum.

Vocals: Powerful vocals welcome. Can be vocal-forward.

Texture: Prominent percussion, heavy bass, crackling highs. Organic + electronic. Electric and alive.

Triggers: Ocimene-dominant, or pinene + effect override, or effect = "energizing/buzzy/invigorating/talkative"

Stage 3: Duration + Tempo Formula

Duration and tempo aren't arbitrary — they're derived from the strain's energy category and the shape of its arc. Longer sessions are for experiences that need time to develop (sleep descent, social gatherings). Shorter sessions are for states that don't need a journey (focus work, bright uplift).

// Duration Formula
IF category = "Deep Rest" → 90 min // needs full iso-principle descent
IF category = "Easy Social" → 75-90 min // social sessions run long
IF category = "Warm Relaxation" → 75-90 min // longer if myrcene > 0.3%
IF category = "Contemplative Drift" → 75-90 min // longer if psychedelic/drift effect
IF category = "Body/Mind Split" → 75 min
IF category = "Clear Focus" → 60 min // shorter = sharper focus
IF category = "Bright Uplift" → 60-70 min // sustained energy, not a marathon
IF category = "Vivid Energy" → 60-75 min // high energy can't sustain 90 min

// Tempo Formula
base_bpm = category.bpm_range
IF limonene > 0.4% → nudge bpm +5
IF myrcene > 0.5% → nudge bpm -5
IF ocimene present → nudge bpm +5
IF bisabolol present → nudge bpm -5
IF arc = "descending" → specify start_bpm AND end_bpm
IF arc = "building" → specify start_bpm AND peak_bpm
The Iso Principle: For sleep/rest playlists, always start where the listener currently is (gentle contentment at ~80 BPM) and gradually guide them down. Never start at silence — that's jarring. The descent should feel inevitable, not forced. This is borrowed from music therapy and is the most critical concept for the Deep Rest category.

Stage 6: Prompt Assembly Template

Now you have all the ingredients. The final step is assembling them into a Spotify prompt using a consistent structure. The prompt architecture follows this sequence — each element flows naturally into the next.

1

Opening: Duration + Core Feeling

"Create a [duration]-minute playlist that [core emotional quality from effect + terpene translation]."

2

Tempo Target

For steady arcs: "Target [BPM range] BPM throughout."
For descending: "Start at [X] BPM and slowly descend to [Y] BPM by the end."
For building: "Start at [X] BPM and build to [Y] by midpoint, then hold."

3

Phase 1 — Opening (first 15-25 min)

"Start with [genre/texture description] — [Artist]'s [specific album/track], [Artist]'s [specific work]."
Always name 2-3 specific artists with album or track references.

4

Phase 2 — Core (middle section)

"Move into / Build into / Settle into [genre/texture] through the middle — [Artist]'s [work], [Artist]'s [work]."
This is the longest section. Name 2-4 artists.

5

Phase 3 — Close (final 20-40 min)

"Close with / Wind down with / Final [X] minutes: [description] — [Artist]'s [work]."
For sleep playlists, this section is the longest and most detailed.

6

Production Notes + Constraints

"[Texture keywords from terpene translation]. [Vocal rule]. [What to avoid]. The vibe is [evocative metaphor]."
Always end with a vivid metaphor that captures the feeling.

The Metaphor Rule: Every prompt ends with a sensory metaphor that ties the music back to the strain experience. This is the most important creative element — it gives Spotify's AI an emotional north star. Draw from the strain's aroma, effect, and name. Examples: "late evening on a porch with a clear head" (Mike's Bomba), "carbonation in your ears" (Carambola), "hammock with zero obligations" (Natty Bumppo).

Worked Example: New Strain → Prompt

Let's walk through the full algorithm with a hypothetical new strain to show how it works end-to-end.

INPUT: "Mango Haze"

Farm: Emerald Triangle, Humboldt
Terpenes: Myrcene 0.62% (dominant), Limonene 0.41%, β-Caryophyllene 0.22%, α-Pinene 0.10%
Effect: Physically relaxed but mentally spacious. Euphoric drift.
Aroma: Mango, pine, sweet earth

Step 1 — Classify: Myrcene dominant (0.62%) but below 1% threshold, so it's not Deep Rest. Limonene is close behind (0.41%), creating a "relaxed body + lifted mind" split. Effect says "physically relaxed but mentally spacious" — this is Contemplative Drift territory (trending toward Body/Mind Split but without the heavy myrcene gravity).

Step 2 — Parameters: Contemplative Drift → BPM 80-100, Energy 0.30-0.50, Duration 90 min (longer because "drift" effect), Arc = Dreamy Wander. Myrcene nudge: -5 BPM → 75-95. Limonene nudge: +5 → 80-95 (net neutral). Pinene adds a touch of clarity.

Step 3 — Texture: Myrcene (moderate) = hazy, golden, reverb-washed. Limonene = bright, sunny top end. Pinene = a touch of electric clarity. Combined: "sun-drenched haze with moments of precision." Vocals: soft first half only.

Step 4 — Artists: Opening (tropical drift): Khruangbin dub, Toro y Moi. Core (dreamy fusion): Azymuth, Marcos Valle, Cortex. Close (ambient drift): Nujabes, Shigeru Suzuki, Monster Rally.

Step 5 — Metaphor: Drawn from mango aroma + drift effect → "floating in a warm tropical current, watching sunlight fracture through the surface."

Generated Prompt Output

Create a 90-minute playlist where the body goes soft and the mind drifts into wide, sun-drenched space. Start around 85 BPM with hazy tropical grooves — Khruangbin's most reverb-drenched Hasta El Cielo dub cuts, Toro y Moi's live Fillmore recordings at their dreamiest. Move into spacious Brazilian-jazz fusion through the middle — Azymuth's cosmic Telecommunication pieces, Marcos Valle's most expansive arrangements, Cortex's Troupeau Bleu with its golden warmth. Weave in a touch of Hiatus Kaiyote's Mood Valiant textures for modern shimmer. Final 30 minutes: pure drift — Nujabes' Spiritual State outros, Shigeru Suzuki's Pacific album shimmer, Monster Rally's tropical collages dissolving into space. Production should feel sun-drenched and slightly melted — heavy reverb, warm saturation, wide stereo field. Soft vocals welcome in the first half if they add to the haze. No sharp edges, no precision, no urgency. The vibe is floating in a warm tropical current, watching sunlight fracture through the surface.

Prompt Quality Checklist

Before submitting a prompt to Spotify's Prompted Playlist feature, run it through these checks.

Structure: Does the prompt specify duration, BPM target(s), and at least 3 playlist phases?

Artists: Are 4-8 artists named, all from Charles's library? Are specific albums/tracks referenced?

Arc: Does the energy shape match the strain's category? Does it describe how the playlist moves through time?

Texture: Are production keywords present that match the terpene profile? (warm/bright/hazy/crisp/etc.)

Vocals: Is the vocal rule explicitly stated? (instrumental only / vocals welcome / vocals first half only)

Constraints: Does the prompt say what to avoid? (no bright tones / no ballads / no minor keys / etc.)

Metaphor: Does it end with a sensory metaphor connecting the music to the strain experience?

Terpene Truth: Would someone consuming this strain and pressing play feel like the music matches their state?
