# Receptor Twins & Terroir Signature Analysis

*23 Solful strains clustered by receptor activation profile, not terpene profile*

## Executive Summary

**5 receptor families** identified across 23 strains.

Two strains can be **receptor twins** — pharmacologically interchangeable — despite having completely different terpene profiles and aromas. This means a customer who loves one strain's *effects* but wants a different *flavor* can switch to its receptor twin.

## 5. Key Insights

### For Customers

- "I love the effects of X but want a different flavor" → check its receptor twin
- Receptor twins share pharmacological outcomes even when terpene profiles differ
- For maximum variety in a session, pair receptor **opposites** (e.g., Carambola + Black Lime Chem)

### For Curation

- **Humboldt** leans toward GABA-A/TRPV1 (sedation, pain) — body-forward
- **Sonoma** leans toward CB2/NF-kB/PPARgamma (anti-inflammatory) — cannabinoid-forward
- **Mendocino** is the most balanced — closest to the global average
- **Trinity** (Glitter Bomb) is the outlier — extreme GABA-A/TRPV1 from high myrcene

### For Menu Design

- Ensure each receptor family has at least one representative on the menu
- Highlight receptor opposites as "adventure pairings"
- Use terroir signatures in marketing: "Humboldt Body" vs "Sonoma Anti-Inflammatory"
