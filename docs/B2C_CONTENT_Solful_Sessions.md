# The Story

## It started with mango juice.

There's an old stoner myth: eat mango before you smoke, and you'll get higher. I heard it back in Montreal in the early 2010s, when cannabis was still illegal and you bought whatever your guy had.

I tried it a few times. Drank mango juice, waited 30 minutes, smoked.

Sometimes it worked. Sometimes nothing happened.

I assumed it was placebo and moved on. But one question stuck with me: **why was it inconsistent?**

---

## The accidental experiment

A few years later, I was deep in business school, chasing perfect grades in finance. I had two brutally hard courses running at the same time: financial accounting and business statistics.

Around then, I came across research on **scent-anchored memory**. The idea was simple: if you study while exposed to a specific scent, and then recreate that scent during an exam, recall improves.

I had gotten into aromatherapy by then—about a dozen essential oils on my desk. So I tested it.

- **Peppermint and lemon** for statistics
- **Rosemary and eucalyptus** for accounting

Every study session, I ran the matching diffuser. During breaks, I'd smoke a little weed—usually Blue Dream or Girl Scout Cookies—just enough to reset before diving back in.

Here's what I noticed without fully understanding it yet: despite having a high tolerance, **I was getting a full, balanced high from very little flower**. And when I went back to studying, I'd fall into deep flow states. Clear. Focused. Locked in.

By midterms, I brought the experiment into the exam room. I dabbed the essential oil blend onto a handkerchief and placed it on my desk.

**100% on statistics. 98% on accounting.**

My first perfect grades ever.

## The eureka moment

The realization came during a study break. Blue Dream in the bong. Procrastinating online. I stumbled back onto the mango myth.

This time, I wanted to understand why it sometimes worked.

The answer came quickly.

**Mango is high in myrcene**—one of the most common terpenes in cannabis. If you eat mango before smoking a myrcene-rich strain, you're stacking the same compound from two sources. The effects add up.

But if the strain doesn't have much myrcene? The mango does nothing.

That explained the inconsistency.

Then it clicked even harder.

I looked up the terpene profiles of the strains in my drawer. There they were: **limonene, linalool, alpha-pinene, myrcene**—the same words printed on the backs of my essential oil bottles.

I looked at my diffuser. I looked at my weed jars. I looked at my essential oils.

**They were the same compounds.**

The molecules that make lavender calming and peppermint energizing? They're the molecules that differentiate Blue Dream from Sour Diesel.

I hadn't just been anchoring memory with scent. I had been **pre-loading my system** with the same terpenes found in the strains I was smoking. That's why I was getting so much effect from so little flower.

The diffuser was priming my brain before the THC even arrived.

# How Terpenes Work

## The molecules behind the magic

You know how different strains feel different—even when the THC percentage is similar?

That's not random. It's chemistry.

**Terpenes** are the aromatic compounds that give cannabis its smell and flavor. They're also in lavender, citrus, pine trees, black pepper—basically any plant with a distinctive scent.

But here's the thing most people don't realize: **terpenes don't just smell good. They do things.**

---

## A quick tour of the key players

### Myrcene — The Relaxer
Found in: mangoes, hops, lemongrass
Effect: sedating, heavy, "couch lock"
The most common terpene in cannabis. High-myrcene strains make you want to melt into the couch.

### Limonene — The Uplifter
Found in: citrus peels, juniper
Effect: mood elevation, energy, alertness
Bright, sunny, optimistic. High-limonene strains tend to feel social and energizing.

### β-Caryophyllene — The Grounder
Found in: black pepper, cloves, rosemary
Effect: stress relief, grounding, warmth
The only terpene proven to directly activate cannabinoid receptors. Spicy and centering.

### Linalool — The Soother
Found in: lavender
Effect: calming, anxiety-reducing, gentle
Floral and soft. The terpene behind lavender's reputation as a relaxation powerhouse.

### Pinene — The Clarifier
Found in: pine needles, rosemary
Effect: alertness, memory, focus
The "clear-headed" terpene. Counteracts some of THC's foggy effects.

### Terpinolene — The Creative
Found in: tea tree, apples, cumin
Effect: uplifting yet calming—a paradox
Associated with strains people describe as "creative" and "cerebral."

---

## Why different strains feel different

Every strain has a unique terpene fingerprint—a specific combination and ratio of these compounds.

**Blue Dream** is high in myrcene and pinene—relaxing but clear.
**Sour Diesel** is high in limonene and caryophyllene—energizing and focused.
**OG Kush** is high in myrcene, limonene, and caryophyllene—the classic "balanced" high.

Same THC. Completely different experiences.

**Terpenes are why.**

---

## The entourage effect

Here's where it gets interesting.

Terpenes don't just add flavor. They **interact with cannabinoids** and change how THC affects you.

Scientists call this the **entourage effect**—the idea that cannabis compounds work better together than alone.

Some examples from the research:
- **Linalool** activates the same brain receptors as anti-anxiety medication
- **Caryophyllene** directly binds to CB2 cannabinoid receptors (the only dietary compound proven to do this)
- **Limonene** can reduce anxiety from THC without blocking the pleasant effects

The flower delivers the cannabinoids. The terpenes shape the ride.

---

## What this means for you

When you choose a strain, you're not just choosing a THC level. You're choosing a terpene profile—a specific cocktail of molecules that will interact with your brain in specific ways.

**Solful Sessions helps you make that choice intentionally**, based on how you actually want to feel.

# The Music Connection

## Why sound matters

Music doesn't just accompany your high. It shapes it.

The same neurological systems that terpenes act on—dopamine, serotonin, GABA—are the systems that music modulates too.

In other words: **terpenes and music are working on the same brain circuitry, through different entry points.**

---

## The science (briefly)

- Music at 60-80 BPM activates your parasympathetic nervous system—the "rest and digest" mode. This is the same state that myrcene and linalool push you toward.

- Upbeat music (100+ BPM, major key) triggers dopamine release in the reward centers of your brain. This is the same pathway that limonene activates.

- Research shows that matching music to mood (then gradually shifting) produces better outcomes than jumping straight to target-mood music. Therapists call this the "iso principle."

# The Aromatherapy Amplifier

## The missing layer

Solful curates strains with intention. Every flower on their menu is chosen for its terpene profile—how it smells, how it feels, what it's good for.

But here's the limitation: **you can only inhale so many terpenes from flower alone.**

The terpene content of cannabis is typically 1-4% of the plant material. And when you smoke or vaporize, some of those terpenes are lost to heat.

**What if you could boost the terpene signal?**

---

## Strain-matched essential oil blends

That's what Solful's aromatherapy blends are designed to do.

Each blend is formulated to mirror the dominant terpene profile of a specific Solful strain—using pure essential oils that contain the same chemical compounds.

**How it works:**

1. Start diffusing the blend 30 minutes before your session
2. Your olfactory system (smell) sends signals directly to your limbic system (emotion, memory)
3. The terpenes begin circulating in your bloodstream through your lungs
4. By the time you consume the flower, your brain is already primed with the same molecules

The effect: faster onset, brighter experience, longer duration—without consuming more cannabis.

**Before (30 min):** Start your diffuser with the strain-matched blend
**During:** Keep diffusing. Optionally add 1-2 drops to water (including bong water)
**After:** Let the diffuser run as you settle into the experience

Simple. Repeatable. No mixing required.
