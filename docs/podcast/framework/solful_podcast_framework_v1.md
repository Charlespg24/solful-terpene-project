# Solful Sessions Podcast — Production Framework

## The Engine: From Terpene Data to Audio Transcript

*A repeatable system for producing podcast-style monologues about individual terpenes, cannabis strains, and terpene profiles.*

---

## Series Identity

**Series Name:** Solful Sessions — The Terpene Files
**Host Voice:** Charles Pelletier-Gagné
**Tagline:** *The same molecule. Every plant. One body.*
**Format:** Solo monologue, 12–20 minutes per episode (2,500–4,000 words)
**Tone:** A smart friend at a dinner party who happens to have spent a decade studying this. Personal, curious, grounded in science but never clinical. Thinks out loud. Uses story to carry pharmacology.

---

## Episode Types

### Type A — Single Terpene Deep-Dive
**Input:** Terpene monograph (like the α-Pinene document)
**Output:** A single-molecule portrait. Origin story, pharmacology, cultural history, cannabis connection, pre-loading application.
**Example:** "Episode 1: α-Pinene — The Forest's Fingerprint"

### Type B — Strain Profile
**Input:** Strain name, COA terpene profile (with %), farm, effects, aroma, lineage
**Output:** A strain portrait told through its terpene ensemble. Each terpene is a character; the profile is the story.
**Example:** "Episode 10: Mike's Bomba — The Grounding Session"

### Type C — Terpene Comparison
**Input:** Two or more terpenes with overlapping or contrasting pharmacology
**Output:** A side-by-side exploration — what they share, where they diverge, why it matters.
**Example:** "Episode 15: Linalool vs. α-Bisabolol — Two Paths to Calm"

## Universal Episode Architecture

Every episode follows a seven-beat structure. Beats can expand or compress depending on content density, but the sequence is fixed. The architecture ensures every episode moves from *sensory* → *scientific* → *cultural* → *practical*.

### Beat 1 — COLD OPEN (60–90 seconds)
**Purpose:** Hook the listener with a sensory scene. No jargon. No definitions.
**Method:** Drop the listener into a place where this terpene lives — a forest, a kitchen, a perfumer's bench, a dispensary counter. Make them smell it before they know its name.
**Rules:**
- First sentence must be a place, a moment, or a question — never a definition
- Name the molecule only at the end of the cold open, as a reveal
- Use present tense for immediacy
- One paragraph max

### Beat 2 — THE MOLECULE (2–3 minutes)
**Purpose:** Make the molecule tangible. What is it? Where does it come from? How abundant is it?
**Method:** Chemical identity translated into human scale. Biosynthesis as craft, not textbook. Botanical sources as a world tour.
**Rules:**
- Lead with the single most surprising fact about this molecule
- Translate molecular weight, boiling point, etc. into sensory or practical terms (not just numbers)
- Botanical source list delivered as geography, not a table — "from the longleaf pines of the American Southeast to the rosemary hillsides of Provence"
- If there are enantiomers or isomers, explain them as *versions* or *dialects*, not chemistry lectures

### Beat 3 — THE SCIENCE (3–5 minutes)
**Purpose:** Pharmacology for the curious. What does this molecule actually do inside the body?
**Method:** Walk through 2–3 mechanisms. Use the "zoom" technique: start at receptor level, zoom out to the experience. Every mechanism gets a "so what" sentence.
**Rules:**
- Maximum 90 seconds of unbroken science before a sensory or narrative beat interrupts
- Lead with the mechanism most relevant to the listener's life (memory, pain, mood, immunity — not the most exotic)
- Always state evidence tier honestly: "This is proven in humans" vs. "This is compelling but so far only in rat models" vs. "This is a hypothesis — elegant, mechanistically sound, but unproven"
- Use the receptor → pathway → experience chain: "It binds here → this cascade happens → you feel/notice this"
- Never say "studies show" without naming who did the study or where

### Beat 4 — THE BRIDGE (2–3 minutes)
**Purpose:** Connect the molecule to everyday life. Humans have been interacting with this terpene for centuries — through food, drink, medicine, ceremony, place.
**Method:** Cultural history as story. Culinary traditions, perfumery, traditional medicine, geographic associations.
**Rules:**
- At least two domains (food + medicine, perfume + ceremony, etc.)
- Name real dishes, real traditions, real products, real places
- Frame ancient use as "they didn't know the chemistry, but they knew the effect"
- This beat should make the listener say "I've been encountering this molecule my whole life and never knew it"

### Beat 5 — THE CANNABIS CONNECTION (2–3 minutes)
**Purpose:** How does this terpene show up in cannabis? What strains carry it? What does it contribute to the experience?
**Method:** Strain examples as case studies. Terpene-to-experience translation.
**Rules:**
- Name 3–5 real strains known for this terpene
- Describe what users report — the "character" this terpene adds to a high
- Connect back to the pharmacology from Beat 3: "That focused clarity Jack Herer is known for? Now you know what's driving it."
- Address the source-independence thesis naturally: "This isn't a cannabis-exclusive molecule. Your body doesn't know or care whether the α-pinene came from Jack Herer or a juniper tree."

### Beat 6 — THE PRE-LOAD (2–3 minutes)
**Purpose:** The Solful Sessions thesis applied. How does temporal pre-loading work with this specific terpene?
**Method:** Walk through the 15–30 minute protocol. Name the botanical vehicles. Explain the timing logic.
**Rules:**
- Frame as "what if you could prime these pathways before you consume?"
- Name specific essential oils and methods (diffuser, direct inhalation, steam, tea)
- Acknowledge this is the frontier — "we're ahead of the clinical trials, but the mechanistic logic is sound"
- Make it actionable: "Here's what you could try tonight"

### Beat 7 — THE CLOSE (60–90 seconds)
**Purpose:** Land the plane. One takeaway the listener carries with them.
**Method:** Return to the sensory scene from the cold open. Recontextualize it with everything they now know.
**Rules:**
- Circle back to Beat 1 — the listener should feel like the episode was a loop, not a line
- End on the terpene source independence principle: same molecule, every plant, one body
- Final sentence should be quotable — the kind of line someone texts to a friend

## Type B Addendum — Strain Profile Episode Structure

For strain episodes, the seven beats adapt:

| Universal Beat | Strain Adaptation |
|---|---|
| COLD OPEN | Describe the *experience* of smoking this strain — the vibe, the setting, the feeling |
| THE MOLECULE | Becomes THE PROFILE — walk through each terpene in the COA as a character in an ensemble cast. Dominant terpene is the lead; supporting terpenes are the cast. |
| THE SCIENCE | Focus on the *interactions* — how do these specific terpenes modulate each other and the cannabinoids? What's the entourage story for this particular ratio? |
| THE BRIDGE | What essential oils, foods, and settings match this strain's terpene voice? |
| CANNABIS CONNECTION | Deep-dive on this specific strain — farm, lineage, growing region, phenotype notes |
| THE PRE-LOAD | The strain-matched essential oil blend recipe. Walk through each oil, why it's there, what it represents. |
| THE CLOSE | The *intent* of the strain — the one-liner that captures its purpose |

## Voice and Style Rules

### DO:
- Speak in first person when sharing personal experience
- Use "you" to address the listener directly
- Mix short punchy sentences with longer flowing ones
- Name real researchers, real studies, real places, real products
- Use metaphor to carry pharmacology: "Think of acetylcholinesterase as the janitor..."
- Pause for emphasis (marked with "..." or "[beat]" in transcript)
- Vary rhythm: fact-story-fact-question-story-fact
- Acknowledge uncertainty — "we don't know yet" is more credible than overclaiming

### DON'T:
- Open with a definition ("Alpha-pinene is a bicyclic monoterpene...")
- Use passive voice for more than one sentence in a row
- Stack more than three scientific terms without a translation beat
- Say "studies show" or "research suggests" without attribution
- Hedge excessively — state the evidence tier once and move on
- Use the word "actually" (overused in science communication)
- End with a generic call to action ("subscribe and leave a review")

### VOCABULARY CALIBRATION:
- **Use freely:** terpene, cannabinoid, receptor, pathway, entourage effect, pre-loading, botanical, volatile
- **Translate on first use:** acetylcholinesterase ("the enzyme that breaks down your memory neurotransmitter"), BDNF ("brain-derived neurotrophic factor — basically, brain fertilizer"), NF-κB ("the master switch for inflammation"), NK cells ("natural killer cells — your immune system's special forces")
- **Avoid entirely in the monologue:** IC₅₀, IUPAC nomenclature, CAS numbers, statistical notation (save these for show notes)

## Production Prompt Template

Use the following prompt structure when generating new episodes. Replace bracketed content with episode-specific data.

```
ROLE: You are writing a podcast monologue transcript for Solful Sessions — The Terpene Files, hosted by Charles Pelletier-Gagné. The series explores terpene science, cannabis pharmacology, and the art of terpene pre-loading for a curious, intelligent audience that is NOT primarily scientific.

EPISODE TYPE: [A: Single Terpene | B: Strain Profile | C: Terpene Comparison]

SOURCE MATERIAL:
[Paste the terpene monograph, COA data, or strain profile here]

EPISODE CONSTRAINTS:
- Target length: [2,500–4,000] words
- Follow the seven-beat structure from the Solful Sessions Production Framework
- Voice: conversational first-person monologue, as if speaking directly to one listener
- Science communication level: informed enthusiast (think Huberman audience, not Nature reviewers)
- Evidence transparency: always state whether evidence is from human studies, animal models, in vitro, or theoretical
- No disclaimers in the body of the monologue (save for show notes section at the end)
- The terpene source independence thesis must appear naturally at least once: the same molecule activates the same receptor regardless of plant origin

COLD OPEN SEED: [Optional — suggest a setting, scene, or sensory hook. Leave blank to let the model choose.]

OUTPUT FORMAT:
1. Episode title
2. Episode number placeholder: "Episode [XX]"
3. Full transcript with beat markers: [COLD OPEN], [THE MOLECULE], [THE SCIENCE], [THE BRIDGE], [THE CANNABIS CONNECTION], [THE PRE-LOAD], [THE CLOSE]
4. SHOW NOTES section after the transcript with:
   - Key studies referenced (full citations)
   - Strains mentioned
   - Essential oils mentioned
   - Pre-loading protocol summary (bullet points OK here)
   - Evidence tier summary table
```
