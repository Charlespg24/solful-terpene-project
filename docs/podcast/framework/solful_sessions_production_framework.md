# Solful Sessions Podcast — Production Framework

## The Engine: From Terpene Data to Audio Transcript

*A repeatable system for producing podcast-style monologues about individual terpenes, cannabis strains, and terpene profiles.*
*Framework version 2.0 — March 2026*

---

## 1. SERIES IDENTITY

**Series Name:** Solful Sessions — The Terpene Files
**Host Voice:** Charles Pelletier-Gagné
**Tagline:** *The same molecule. Every plant. One body.*
**Format:** Solo monologue, 18–25 minutes per episode (3,000–4,500 words)
**Tone:** A smart friend at a dinner party who happens to have spent a decade studying this. Personal, curious, grounded in science but never clinical. Thinks out loud. Uses story to carry pharmacology.

---

## 2. EPISODE TYPES

### Type A — Single Terpene Deep-Dive
**Input:** Terpene monograph
**Output:** A single-molecule portrait. Origin story, pharmacology, cultural history, cannabis connection, pre-loading application.
**Example:** "Episode 01: α-Pinene — The Forest's Fingerprint"

### Type B — Strain Profile
**Input:** Strain COA + farm context + personal experience
**Output:** Strain portrait told through its terpene ensemble. Each terpene is a character; the profile is the story. Includes essential oil recipe, beverage pairing, music, and ritual context.
**Example:** "Episode 10: Mike's Bomba — The Grounding Session"

### Type C — Terpene Comparison
**Input:** Two or more terpenes with overlapping or contrasting pharmacology
**Output:** Side-by-side exploration — what they share, where they diverge, why it matters.
**Example:** "Episode 15: Linalool vs. α-Bisabolol — Two Paths to Calm"

### Type D — Farm Series
**Input:** Multiple strains from one farm
**Output:** Farm philosophy + daily arc showing how one soil and one philosophy produce different terpene expressions across strains.

---

## 3. SEVEN-BEAT EPISODE ARCHITECTURE

Every episode follows this sequence. Beats expand or compress, but the order is fixed: sensory → scientific → cultural → practical.

| Beat | Duration | Purpose | Rules |
|------|----------|---------|-------|
| **COLD OPEN** | 60–90 sec | Hook with a sensory scene. No jargon. Name the molecule at the END as a reveal. | First sentence = place, moment, or question — never a definition. Present tense. |
| **THE MOLECULE** | 2–3 min | Make the molecule tangible. Chemical identity as human-scale story. Botanical sources as geography. | Lead with the most surprising fact. Translate numbers into sensory terms. |
| **THE SCIENCE** | 3–5 min | Pharmacology for the curious. 2–3 mechanisms. Receptor → pathway → experience chain. | Lead with strongest evidence, not most famous claim. State evidence tier honestly. Max 90 sec of unbroken science before a narrative beat. Always name who did the study. |
| **THE BRIDGE** | 2–3 min | Cultural history. Food, drink, medicine, ceremony, place. | At least two domains. Frame ancient use as "they didn't know the chemistry, but they knew the effect." |
| **CANNABIS CONNECTION** | 2–3 min | How this terpene shows up in cannabis. Strains as case studies. | Name 3–5 real strains. Connect user reports back to pharmacology. State source independence naturally. |
| **THE PRE-LOAD** | 2–3 min | Practical protocol. Essential oils, methods, timing. | Name specific oils. Acknowledge this is the frontier. Make it actionable. State bioavailability constraints honestly. |
| **THE CLOSE** | 60–90 sec | Return to cold open scene. Recontextualize with everything they now know. | Circle back to Beat 1. End on source independence. Final sentence should be quotable. |

### Type B Strain Adaptation

| Universal Beat | Strain Adaptation |
|---|---|
| COLD OPEN | Describe the *experience* of smoking this strain — the vibe, the setting, the feeling |
| THE MOLECULE | Becomes THE PROFILE — walk through each terpene in the COA as a character in an ensemble cast. Dominant terpene is the lead; supporting terpenes are the cast. |
| THE SCIENCE | Focus on the *interactions* — how do these specific terpenes modulate each other and the cannabinoids? What's the entourage story for this particular ratio? |
| THE BRIDGE | What essential oils, foods, and settings match this strain's terpene voice? |
| CANNABIS CONNECTION | Deep-dive on this specific strain — farm, lineage, growing region, phenotype notes |
| THE PRE-LOAD | The strain-matched essential oil blend recipe. Walk through each oil, why it's there, what it represents. |
| THE CLOSE | The *intent* of the strain — the one-liner that captures its purpose |

## 4. VOICE & STYLE RULES

### DO:
- Speak in first person for personal experience
- Use "you" to address the listener directly
- Mix short punchy sentences with longer flowing ones
- Name real researchers, real studies, real places, real products
- Use metaphor to carry pharmacology: "Think of acetylcholinesterase as the janitor..."
- Pause for emphasis (marked with "..." or "[beat]" in transcript)
- Vary rhythm: fact-story-fact-question-story-fact
- Acknowledge uncertainty — "we don't know yet" is more credible than overclaiming

### DON'T:
- Open with a definition ("Alpha-pinene is a bicyclic monoterpene...")
- Use passive voice for more than one sentence in a row
- Stack more than three scientific terms without a translation beat
- Say "studies show" or "research suggests" without attribution
- Hedge excessively — state the evidence tier once and move on
- Use the word "actually" (overused in science communication)
- End with a generic call to action ("subscribe and leave a review")

### VOCABULARY CALIBRATION:
- **Use freely:** terpene, cannabinoid, receptor, pathway, entourage effect, pre-loading, botanical, volatile
- **Translate on first use:** acetylcholinesterase ("the enzyme that breaks down your memory neurotransmitter"), BDNF ("brain-derived neurotrophic factor — basically, brain fertilizer"), NF-κB ("the master switch for inflammation"), NK cells ("natural killer cells — your immune system's special forces")
- **Avoid entirely in the monologue:** IC₅₀, IUPAC nomenclature, CAS numbers, statistical notation (save these for show notes)

## 5. EVIDENCE TRANSPARENCY TIERS

Every scientific claim in the monologue gets one of these labels:

| Tier | Label | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Human RCT (gold standard) | Spindle 2024 limonene trial |
| 2 | Human observational / clinical | Qing Li NK cell forest bathing studies |
| 3 | Animal model (strong) | Gertsch 2008 CB2 knockout mice |
| 4 | In vitro / cell culture | Receptor binding assays |
| 5 | Mechanistic hypothesis | A2a-CB1 heteromer crosstalk |
| 6 | Tested negative | α-Pinene memory protection (Hopkins) |

**Credibility signature:** Always state when evidence is strong AND when it failed. "If I told you only the Russo hypothesis and left out the Hopkins data, I'd sound like every other cannabis podcast. I'd rather tell you the whole story and let you decide what it means."

Use this when generating new episodes:

```

EPISODE TYPE: [A: Single Terpene | B: Strain Profile | C: Comparison | D: Farm Series]

SOURCE MATERIAL:
[Paste the terpene monograph, COA data, strain profile, or farm context]

EPISODE CONSTRAINTS:
- Target: 3,000–4,500 words (~20–25 minutes)
- Seven-beat structure (Cold Open, The Molecule, The Science, The Bridge, The Cannabis Connection, The Pre-Load, The Close)
- Voice: conversational first-person monologue
- Science level: informed enthusiast (Huberman audience, not Nature reviewers)
- Evidence transparency: always state tier (human RCT, animal model, in vitro, theoretical, tested-negative)
- Source independence thesis appears naturally at least once
- No disclaimers in body (save for show notes)
- Lead Science section with strongest evidence, not most famous claim
- Always read the full monograph before writing — don't rely on project knowledge alone

COLD OPEN SEED: [Optional setting/scene suggestion]

OUTPUT: Episode title, transcript with beat markers, Show Notes (citations, strains, oils, protocol summary, evidence tier table)
```

## 12. THREE-EPISODE LAUNCH STRATEGY

Don't launch with one episode. Write and produce Episodes 01 (α-Pinene), 02 (β-Caryophyllene), and 03 (Limonene) before publishing anything. Trailer drops first, 3–5 day gap, then all three go live simultaneously. This gives new listeners enough to binge, signals commitment to the algorithms, and buys a month of runway before Episode 04 is needed.

The strategic arc across the trilogy:

- **Episode 01 (α-Pinene)** — The honest one. Strongest evidence is NK cells/forest bathing. The famous memory claim got tested and failed. Establishes credibility through transparency.
- **Episode 02 (β-Caryophyllene)** — The deepest one. Three receptor systems. The regulatory paradox. Drug-sniffing dogs. Most pharmacological mechanisms of any episode.
- **Episode 03 (d-Limonene)** — The proof. The one episode where a terpene-cannabinoid interaction has been demonstrated in a human RCT. Everything the first two episodes built toward pays off here.
