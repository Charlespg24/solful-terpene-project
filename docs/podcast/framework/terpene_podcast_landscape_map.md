## CRITICAL INTEL — READ FIRST

Before the landscape map, something important surfaced in this research that directly impacts Episode 01 and the broader thesis:

**Johns Hopkins tested α-pinene against THC memory impairment — and found NO evidence supporting the claim.**

Per a January 2025 GreenState interview with Ryan Vandrey: "Building on this research [the limonene/anxiety study], the Lab conducted a similar experiment to test whether alpha-pinene could counteract the memory-impairing effects of high doses of THC. However, according to Professor Vandrey, they found no evidence supporting this claim."

This is directly relevant to the Russo 2011 AChE hypothesis that anchors the α-pinene memory narrative. The Episode 01 transcript already hedges this carefully ("It's a hypothesis. A very good one. But a hypothesis."), but you should know the actual clinical data is now negative. The limonene/anxiety study worked; the pinene/memory one did not — at least at the doses and routes tested. Vandrey himself cautioned these are "singular one-off experiments" needing replication at different doses and routes.

**Implication for your work:** The terpene source independence thesis and pre-loading framework are NOT undermined — the limonene result validates the core mechanism. But the α-pinene/memory claim specifically needs to be presented with even more epistemic humility. This is also an opportunity: you can position Solful Sessions as more honest than anyone else in the space.

## THE GAP — Where Solful Sessions Lives

After reviewing every podcast that covers terpenes, here is the landscape truth:

**Nobody is doing what you're doing.**

The existing podcast world falls into three silos that never talk to each other:

1. **Cannabis podcasts** that mention terpenes as a subtopic of weed
2. **Aromatherapy podcasts** that discuss essential oils without connecting to cannabis
3. **Academic interviews** where researchers appear as guests on other people's shows

No one has built a **terpene-first, cross-botanical, science-grounded series** that treats the molecule as the protagonist — not cannabis, not essential oils, not forest bathing — the molecule itself, across all its sources. That's the white space.

## TIER 1 — Terpene-Dedicated Podcasts

These are the only shows where terpenes are the primary subject, not a guest topic.

### Talking Terpenes
- **Host:** Gary Nickel
- **Platform:** Apple Podcasts, Spotify
- **Focus:** Medical practitioners; how terpenes can broaden treatment options
- **Angle:** Clinical/medical — aimed at healthcare providers wanting to add terpenes to their practice
- **Status:** Launched ~2020, appears intermittently active
- **Relevance to you:** Medical framing only. No cross-botanical narrative. No cannabis consumer angle. No pre-loading.

### Terpenes and Testing Podcast
- **Host:** Dr. Jason Lupoi, PhD (Editor in Chief)
- **Platform:** Amazon Music, Apple Podcasts, Player FM
- **Format:** Interview-based; conversations with scientists and industry professionals
- **Notable guests:** Dr. Jeff Raber (The Werc Shop), Avery Gilbert PhD (olfactory science/Headspace Sensory), Dr. Hope Jones (Emergent Crop Sciences), Dr. Nigam Arora (Marcu & Arora consulting)
- **Angle:** Cannabis testing, lab science, analytical chemistry — terpenes through the lens of quality control
- **Relevance to you:** Technical but narrow. No consumer narrative. No aromatherapy bridge. Good source for potential guest contacts.

### Terpene Therapy
- **Host:** Unnamed host (cannabis culture personality)
- **Platform:** Apple Podcasts, YouTube, Patreon
- **Focus:** Cannabis dab culture; strain reviews; terpene profiles of concentrates
- **Status:** 100+ episodes, active through 2025
- **Angle:** Consumer/culture — "terp hunting" community. Entertainment-first.
- **Relevance to you:** Opposite end of the spectrum. Pure cannabis culture, no science rigor, no cross-botanical connection.

### Terp Trends
- **Host:** Unnamed
- **Platform:** Apple Podcasts
- **Focus:** Cannabis industry trends, with emphasis on terpene preservation, extraction, and hash-making
- **Angle:** Industry insider — cultivators, processors, hash makers
- **Relevance to you:** B2B cannabis focus. No consumer science education.

## TIER 2 — Podcasts with Significant Terpene Episodes

These are broader shows where terpenes have received dedicated episodes or recurring attention.

### Curious About Cannabis / Isn't Life Curious?
- **Host:** Jason Wilson, MS (biologist/science educator)
- **Platform:** Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Blubrry
- **Key terpene episodes:**
  - BTS #05: Dr. Ethan Russo on CBD, Entourage Effects, Terpenes
  - BTS #28: Jacqueline von Salm PhD on Cannabis Terpenes, Chemical Ecology
  - BTS #78: Adie Rae PhD — "Cannabis Quality is More Than THC or Terpenes"
  - 2025: "Cannabis Quality, Aroma, and Terpenes — What Do We Really Know?" with TJ Martin, Iain Oswald, Kyle Boyar, Evan Friedmann (recorded at Abstrax HQ after ACS conference)
  - Kevin Spelman PhD on "Herbs to Complement or Counteract Cannabis Effects"
- **Angle:** Deep science education. Critical thinking. Evidence-based.
- **Relevance to you:** HIGH. This is the closest to your intellectual level but framed as cannabis science, not terpene-first. Jason Wilson could be a great peer/collaborator. The Kevin Spelman episode on herbs complementing cannabis is very close to your thesis.

### Cannabis Cultivation and Science Podcast (KiS Organics)
- **Host:** Tad Hussey
- **Key terpene episode:** Episode 97 — "The Importance of Terpenes and the Entourage Effect" with Dr. Ethan Russo (June 2022)
- **Angle:** Cultivation science; Russo as expert guest
- **Relevance to you:** One landmark episode. Russo discusses his terpene-cannabinoid interaction research directly.

### Shaping Fire
- **Host:** Shango Los
- **Key terpene episodes:**
  - Episode 11: "Cannabinoids and Terpenoids in Detail" with Dr. Ethan Russo
  - Episode 27: Part 2 with Dr. Ethan Russo
  - Episode 18: "Putting Terpenes to Work for You" with Ben Cassiday
  - Episode 17: "Evaluating Cannabis Quality" with Max Montrose
- **Angle:** Cannabis science and culture interviews
- **Relevance to you:** Russo's appearance here is one of the most-cited podcast interviews in the terpene space.

### The Cannabis Conversation
- **Host:** Anuj Desai (UK-based cannabis entrepreneur/lawyer)
- **Key terpene episode:** Episode 61 — "Cannabis and Terpenes" with Dr. Ethan Russo
- **Angle:** European cannabis industry perspective
- **Key quote from episode:** Russo describes his then-upcoming research with Vandrey/Spindle: "Using memory tests as a measure" of terpene-THC interactions. This was the research that eventually produced both the limonene result (positive) and the α-pinene result (negative).

### Reefer Medness
- **Host:** Trevor (pharmacist, Canada-based)
- **Key terpene episode:** E137 — "Ethan Russo - Anxiety, Limonene & CBG" (Jan 2025)
- **Angle:** Medical cannabis for healthcare practitioners
- **Key content:** Russo discusses the landmark Spindle et al. 2024 limonene RCT in detail. Also mentions upcoming myrcene study and oral limonene replication.

### Mind & Matter
- **Host:** Nick Jikomes
- **Key terpene episode:** Episode 152 — "Plant Chemistry, Cannabinoids, Cannabis Terpenes & the Entourage Effect" with Ryan Vandrey PhD
- **Angle:** Science/neuroscience podcast
- **Relevance to you:** HIGH. This is Vandrey himself discussing limonene, myrcene, pinene, and the entourage effect. One of the few podcasts where the actual researcher (not a journalist interpreting the research) speaks.

### Lit & Lucid Cannabis Podcast
- **Host:** Unnamed
- **Key terpene episodes:**
  - E.109: "The Future of Terpenes" with Max Koby (Abstrax Tech CEO)
  - E.110: "The Art & Science of Interpening" with Max Montrose (Trichome Institute)
  - E.025: "Cannabis Education" with Emma Chasen
- **Angle:** Cannabis industry interviews
- **Relevance to you:** Koby/Abstrax represents the terpene manufacturing/extraction side. Montrose/Trichome Institute represents the sensory evaluation ("interpening") side.

### Cannabis Science 101 / Periodic Effects
- **Host:** Linked to Periodic Effects app
- **Recurring guest:** Emma Chasen (multiple episodes)
- **Terpene content:** THC% vs terpene profile debates; how terpenes affect edibles vs. inhalation; lifecycle of cannabis compounds post-harvest
- **Angle:** Consumer education — terpenes as part of "cannabis literacy"

### Enhance Your Life Podcast
- **Key terpene episode:** "What Are Terpenes, Exactly?" with Maxwell Koby, CEO of Abstrax (April 2022)
- **Angle:** General wellness audience introduced to terpenes

### Ganjapreneur Podcast
- **Key episodes:** Multiple interviews with Dr. Ethan Russo, including deep discussion of endocannabinoid deficiency and terpene pharmacology
- **Angle:** Cannabis business + science

### doTERRA Podcast
- **Key terpene episode:** Episode 274 — "Introduction to Shinrin-Yoku" with Dr. Brannick Riggs
- **Angle:** Essential oil product marketing, but with real forest bathing science
- **Key content:** Dr. Riggs explicitly names alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, linalool, limonene, and 1,8-cineole as the phytoncides responsible for forest bathing effects. Directly cites Qing Li's research. Discusses "reverse-engineering" the forest atmosphere with essential oil blends.
- **Relevance to you:** HIGH. doTERRA already created a Shinrin-Yoku essential oil blend built on the same phytoncide science you're citing. They're doing it from the aromatherapy side without the cannabis connection. Your thesis bridges their world and the cannabis world.

### Cal Poly Humboldt — Subsistence Crop
- **Key terpene episode:** S1E1 with Dr. Ethan Russo
- **Key content:** Russo specifically describes alpha-pinene producing "a clear high, where somebody could be very high, but still concentrate." Also discusses the Vandrey/Hopkins work, esters, thiols, and the limits of current knowledge.

## TIER 3 — Aromatherapy/Essential Oil Podcasts

These cover terpenes through the essential oil lens, without cannabis connection.

### Essential Aromatica
- **Host:** Amy Anthony (certified aromatherapist, herbalist, distiller)
- **Angle:** Botanical chemistry and aromatic compounds' effects on physiology
- **Relevance to you:** Close philosophical alignment — "aromatic molecules are chemical messages influencing our emotions, physiology, and state of mind." Could be a collaborator or cross-promotion opportunity.

### The Essential Oil Revolution
- **Host:** Dr. Sarah LoBisco / Samantha Lee Wright
- **Platform:** Apple Podcasts (#1 essential oils podcast by claim)
- **Angle:** Consumer wellness; essential oils for health, emotional wellness, DIY
- **Relevance to you:** Massive audience but wellness-consumer framing. No cannabis. No pharmacology rigor.

### Vetiver Vibes (Essentria)
- **Hosts:** Nikki Fraser, Rachael Dean
- **Angle:** Aromatherapist certification, business building

### Heretic Parfum Podcast
- **Key episode:** Forest Bathing with Ben Page (forest therapy guide, author of *Healing Trees*)
- **Key content:** Page describes phytoncides, NK cell activation, the dual meaning of "bathing" — literal (phytoncide atmosphere) and metaphorical (sunbathing-like presence)
- **Relevance to you:** Perfumery + forest bathing + terpene science. Overlaps with your La Séance sensory framing.
