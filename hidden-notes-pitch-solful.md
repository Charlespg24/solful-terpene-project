## The Industry Is Moving

### From THC percentages to terpene profiles

**Leafly dropped Indica/Sativa** — The world's largest cannabis platform abandoned the indica/sativa framework in favor of terpene-based classification. They know the future is flavor, not category.

**Emerald Cup judges on terpenes** — Cannabis industry's most prestigious competition now evaluates entries on terpene profiles, not just THC content. The best growers already know this.

**Consumers are getting smarter** — Educated customers ask for terpene profiles, not just strain names. They want to understand why one strain relaxes and another energizes.

**Solful is already ahead** — Your Health & Happiness consultations, your sun-grown curation, your "Meet the Farmer" events — you've been building toward terpene-first education. Hidden Notes takes it to the table.

> *Hidden Notes makes terpene education experiential — and positions Solful at the center of it.*

## The Experience

Five courses. Five terpenes. Each paired with food, cocktail, essential oil diffusion, and music.

**Course I — The Awakening** (α-Pinene)
Salmon Gravlax, Fennel & Blood Orange / Gin & Rosemary Fizz

**Course II — The Bloom** (Linalool)
Petrale Sole, Dry Vermouth & Tarragon / Lavender French 75

**Course III — The Heart** (Myrcene)
Mushroom Vol-Au-Vent, Thyme Reduction / Mango-Lemongrass Smash

**Course IV — The Spice** (β-Caryophyllene)
Hanger Steak, Black Pepper Sauce / Mezcal & Black Pepper Old Fashioned

**Course V — The Glow** (Limonene)
Pistachio Crème Brûlée, Blood Orange Zest / Limoncello Spritz

**The Return** — Custom herbal tea containing all five terpenes reunited. Tasting cards revealed: which Solful strain matches each course.

No cannabis is consumed at Caché. The terpenes are the bridge.

## The Customer Journey

### 1. The Dinner
Guests experience five courses, each built around a terpene. They taste it in the food, smell it in the air, feel the mood shift in the music. They learn what pinene, linalool, myrcene, caryophyllene, and limonene actually do — through their senses.

### 2. The Reveal
During the herbal tea finale, tasting cards are flipped. The back of each card names the Solful strain that shares that course's terpene profile. Guests realize: "That calming lavender course? That's the same terpene in Lavender Kush. And it's available at Solful, 1.5 blocks from here."

### 3. The Visit
Guests arrive at Solful already speaking the language. They don't ask for "something relaxing" — they ask for strains high in linalool. Your Health & Happiness consultants can go deeper, faster.

### 4. The Relationship
These aren't one-time visitors. They're educated customers who understand terpene profiles and will return for specific strains. They tell friends. They share tasting cards on social media. They become the kind of customer Solful was built for.
